Interactive Documentary

 Interactive documentaries are more fun than traditional videos, using interactive game options to neutralize the seriousness and educational nature of documentaries. I chose to make an interactive documentary about a day trip to hell precisely because I wanted to use the fun of interactive documentaries to dissolve the boring and dogmatic feeling of popular science. I use this format to explore the combination of Eastern culture and modern expression in this project.

 The biggest advantage of the branching structure in this documentary is that it increases the audience's participation and freedom. By dividing the exploration into distinct areas, viewers can choose which scenes to engage with, minimizing fatigue from repetitive or uninteresting content. In a virtual world, immersion is stronger when users can influence the narrative, making the learning experience more memorable and effective (Ryan, 2015).

 To tell this story, I primarily used an participatory mode. Although I cited historical documents common in expository documentaries, I focused more on engaging the audience through the interactive characters in Difu Tour, who are both narrators and participants in the story. These characters not only provide narrative, but also allow the audience to make choices that affect the direction of the story, thereby enhancing the overall immersion and participation (Nichols, 2010). However, the story unfolds under my subjective guidance. This approach maintains narrative coherence while fully reflecting interactivity, and strikes a balance between the two.

 In addition, I also referenced some Poetic Mode and used some musical effects and visual language to shape the atmosphere of the documentary. For example, the soundtracks and screams of the Yellow Springs Road and the Trial of Becoming a Ghost can make the audience feel the frightening atmosphere; and I use warm and clean images when showing the Lookout and the festivals of the hell, while I use gloomy and blurred images at other times.

 By combining these elements, the documentary balances entertainment and education, providing an engaging and memorable exploration of Eastern cultural themes in a modern, interactive format.
